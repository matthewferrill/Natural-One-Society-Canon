# Design Decisions

Natural One Society Design Decisions Archive

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

## Purpose

The Design Decisions archive preserves the reasoning behind significant project decisions.

The purpose of Design Decisions is to document why important choices were made, what alternatives were considered, and what reasoning led to the final outcome.

The Design Decisions archive is a historical resource.

The Design Decisions archive does not establish canon.

Authority remains with approved Canon documents and Operating Procedures.

The Design Decisions archive helps answer:

* Why was a decision made?
* What alternatives were considered?
* What problems were being solved?
* What tradeoffs were evaluated?
* What reasoning led to the final outcome?

## Decision Categories

Natural One Society design decisions may include:

### Repository Decisions

Repository structure, organization, navigation, and preservation systems.

### Workflow Decisions

Process design, governance procedures, and operational workflows.

### Canon Decisions

Decisions affecting canon structure, organization, and management.

### Character Development Decisions

Decisions affecting character creation, development, and documentation.

### Asset Management Decisions

Decisions affecting visual assets, authority hierarchy, and asset organization.

## Design Decision Usage

Design Decisions should be created when:

* A significant project decision is made.
* Multiple alternatives are considered.
* A decision may require future explanation.
* A decision affects repository structure.
* A decision affects project workflow.
* A decision establishes a new standard or practice.

Design Decisions preserve reasoning.

Design Decisions do not establish authority.

Approved authority remains within Canon documents and Operating Procedures.

## Navigation

Use Design Decisions when researching:

* Why a process exists
* Why a workflow was adopted
* Why repository structures were created
* Why standards were established
* Why alternative approaches were rejected

The Design Decisions archive should be consulted when understanding the reasoning behind important project choices.

## Core Philosophy

Decisions should be understandable.

Reasoning should be preserved.

Future contributors should not be required to rediscover solved problems.

The goal is not merely to preserve outcomes.

The goal is to preserve understanding.

The Design Decisions archive preserves project reasoning.
