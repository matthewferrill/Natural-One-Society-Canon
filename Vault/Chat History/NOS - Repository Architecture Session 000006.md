Incorrect CFG Scale and wrong VAE are the most common causes of persistent blurriness in ComfyUI with Flux models. Flux is highly sensitive to guidance settings; using a CFG scale above 4.5 (e.g., defaults like 7.0 or 8.0) causes over-saturation and loss of detail, resulting in a blurry or "fried" appearance. 

Additionally, ensure you are using the correct VAE (specifically ae.safetensors) and avoiding the Karras scheduler, which is known to produce soft or blurry outputs with Flux. 

Key Fixes for Blurry Flux Outputs in ComfyUI
CFG Scale Adjustment Reduce the CFG/Scale to the 3.5–4.5 range. Flux models are designed for low guidance; higher values crush contrast and obscure fine details. 

Scheduler and Sampler Selection Switch from Karras to Beta or Simple schedulers. Use the Euler or Heun sampler instead of DDPM or specialized GPU samplers that may require higher step counts than your workflow provides. 

Step Count Optimization While Flux Schnell is optimized for 4–8 steps, Flux Dev typically requires 15–20 steps for clarity. Using too few steps with Dev can result in under-converged, soft images. 

VAE Verification Explicitly load the ae.safetensors VAE associated with Flux. Mismatched VAEs (e.g., using SDXL VAE on Flux) cause washed-out, blurry, or color-shifted outputs. 

Resolution and Prompting Ensure resolution is 1024x1024 or higher (multiples of 64). Avoid the token "background" in prompts, as it has been reported to induce blurriness in Flux generations. 

Upscaling for Detail If the base generation is soft, use SUPIR or Real-ESRGAN for upscaling. When upscaling in latent space, keep denoise between 0.3–0.5; a denoise of 1.0 destroys the image structure.
