\# Healer Character History



Natural One Society Vault



Status: Historical Summary

Source: NOS Planning Chat 000001

Canon Status: Not Yet Approved



\## Overview



The Healer is one of the original planned core party members of Natural One Society.



Unlike the Kinder and Orc, the Healer does not yet have an approved Character Bible.



However, multiple discussions establish the intended role of the Healer within the party and the broader Natural One Society universe.



The Healer is considered a future core cast member.



\---



\## Original Purpose



When the Natural One Society Style Bible was expanded to include future characters, the Healer was reserved as one of the major missing emotional pillars.



At that time the Healer was intentionally left undefined.



The stated purpose was:



\* Add a new emotional dimension to the party.

\* Occupy a role not already covered by:



&#x20; \* Kinder

&#x20; \* Orc

&#x20; \* Bard

\* Receive a future Character Bible.



The archive repeatedly emphasizes that future characters should contribute unique emotional roles rather than duplicate existing ones.

\## Emerging Character Concept



Across multiple discussions, a pattern begins to emerge.



The Healer appears to be envisioned as:



\* Good-hearted

\* Helpful

\* Caring

\* Well-intentioned



However:



\* Things frequently go wrong.

\* Attempts to help often create additional problems.

\* The Healer's failures are humorous rather than malicious.



This fits the broader Natural One Society philosophy:



Failure creates stories.



Failure does not define the character.



\---



\## Running Gag Indicators



Several archive references suggest recurring Healer jokes.



\### Campfire Moment #001



The Bard's lute string snaps.



The Dwarf is struck in the eye.



The Healer rushes in to help.



The Healer accidentally knocks the Dwarf's hammer into the campfire.



This establishes a recurring pattern:



The Healer responds immediately.



The situation becomes worse.



Everyone remains friends.



\---



\### Kinder Relationship



The Kinder Character Bible contains one established relationship note:



"He frequently acquires healing supplies."



Usually from the Healer's pockets.



This implies:



\* The Healer carries many supplies.

\* The Kinder regularly "borrows" them.

\* The Healer is likely accustomed to this behavior.



Potential future running gag:



The Healer never has the item needed because the Kinder already has it.

\## Working Thesis



Based on all surviving references:



The Healer appears to be developing toward:



"A person whose desire to help exceeds their ability to execute."



They are not incompetent.



They are not malicious.



They are not reckless.



They simply create unintended consequences while trying to do the right thing.



Their failures are likely to generate:



\* Comedy

\* Character moments

\* Friendship moments



rather than punishment.
