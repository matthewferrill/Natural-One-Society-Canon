# Retrospectives

Natural One Society Retrospectives Archive

Status: Active

Version: 1.0

## Purpose

The Retrospectives archive preserves lessons learned, observations, project reflections, and opportunities for improvement.

Retrospectives exist to help Natural One Society improve over time through reflection and continuous learning.

The Retrospectives archive is a historical resource.

The Retrospectives archive does not establish canon.

Authority remains with approved Canon documents and Operating Procedures.

The Retrospectives archive helps answer:

* What worked well?
* What did not work well?
* What lessons were learned?
* What should be repeated?
* What should be improved?
* What should be avoided in the future?

## Retrospective Categories

Natural One Society retrospectives may include:

### Project Retrospectives

Reviews of major project milestones and outcomes.

### Workflow Retrospectives

Reviews of processes, procedures, and operational workflows.

### Repository Retrospectives

Reviews of repository structure, organization, and preservation systems.

### Character Development Retrospectives

Reviews of character creation and development processes.

### Session Retrospectives

Reviews of individual work sessions and project activities.

## Retrospective Usage

Retrospectives should be created when:

* Significant milestones are completed.
* Important lessons are learned.
* Processes are refined.
* Improvements are identified.
* Major projects conclude.
* Future guidance would benefit from reflection.

Retrospectives preserve lessons learned.

Retrospectives do not establish authority.

Approved authority remains within Canon documents and Operating Procedures.

## Navigation

Use Retrospectives when researching:

* Lessons learned
* Process improvements
* Historical observations
* Project milestones
* Workflow effectiveness
* Continuous improvement opportunities

The Retrospectives archive should be consulted when evaluating how the project can improve over time.

## Core Philosophy

Experience has value.

Lessons should be preserved.

Successes should be repeated.

Mistakes should be understood.

Improvement should be intentional.

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is continuous improvement.

The Retrospectives archive preserves project wisdom.
