Alignment Notes
The Kinder genuinely wants good outcomes and cares about other people, but has little regard for rules, property ownership, social conventions, plans, schedules, or common sense.
His actions are guided by curiosity, compassion, and impulse rather than structure or authority.
The Kinder is not:
•	Chaotic Neutral 
•	A prankster 
•	A criminal 
•	Selfish 
The Kinder is:
•	Kind 
•	Generous 
•	Helpful 
•	Compassionate 
His chaos comes from distraction and curiosity, not malice.

Law #4
The Kinder's Successes Are Charisma Based
The Kinder is fortunate, but he is not a luck-driven character.
People help him because his charisma is extraordinarily high.
They:
•	Rescue him from danger. 
•	Forgive his mistakes. 
•	Give him second chances. 
•	Offer him help. 
•	Give him what he wants. 
Even when they probably shouldn't.
The Kinder's greatest strength is not luck, stealth, intelligence, strength, or skill.
It is his ability to inspire affection, goodwill, and protectiveness in others.
This works because the Kinder is fundamentally good.
He does not manipulate people.
He does not exploit kindness.
He does not act from greed, anger, fear, jealousy, cruelty, or spite.
People help him because they genuinely want to.
Character Interpretation
The Kinder does not succeed despite failing.
The Kinder succeeds because people choose to help him after he fails.
People are aware of the Kinder's habits.
Friends, allies, merchants, and companions often take reasonable precautions to protect their belongings.
These precautions are usually unsuccessful.
